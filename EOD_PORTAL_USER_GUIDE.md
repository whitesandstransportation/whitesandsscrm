## 🎯 What Is This System?

The **DAR EOD Portal** (Daily Activity Report - End of Day Portal) is your daily work tracking companion. Think of it as a sophisticated time clock combined with a task manager and productivity assistant. It helps you:

- Track exactly how long you work on each task
- Work with multiple clients throughout your day
- Organize your tasks before you start them
- Get gentle reminders to stay on track
- Submit professional daily reports to your team

---

## 🏗️ How The System Is Organized

### The Big Picture

Imagine a restaurant with different stations:

1. **Clock-In Station** - Where you "punch in" for each client
2. **Task Station** - Where you actively work on tasks
3. **Queue Station** - Where you prepare tasks before starting them
4. **Template Kitchen** - Where you save recipes for tasks you do often
5. **History Archive** - Where you review everything you've done
6. **Dashboard** - Where you (or your manager) see performance insights

## 🕐 Part 1: Clocking In & Out

### How Clock-In Works

**What You See:**
- A list of your clients
- A "Clock In" button next to each client name
- A timer showing how long you've been clocked in

**What Happens When You Clock In:**
1. You click "Clock In" for a specific client (e.g., "Acme Corp")
2. The system records the exact time you started
3. A mood check popup appears asking "How are you feeling?"
4. The timer starts running in the corner of your screen
5. You're now ready to start working on tasks for that client

**Important Notes:**
- You can be clocked in for multiple clients at once
- Each client has its own separate clock-in
- If you forget to clock out, the system automatically clocks you out after 12 hours
- Your time is tracked in EST (Eastern Standard Time) for consistency

### The Mood & Energy Check-Ins

**Why They Exist:**
These help the system understand your productivity patterns and give you personalized insights.

**How They Work:**
- **First Check:** Appears 2 seconds after you clock in
- **Regular Checks:** Popup every 30 minutes while you're working
- **What It Asks:**
  - "How's your mood?" (Happy, Neutral, Stressed, etc.)
  - "How's your energy?" (High, Medium, Low)

**What Happens With This Data:**
- Stored securely in your personal record
- Used to create insights like "You work best between 9-11am"
- Never shared with others unless you're viewing your dashboard
- Helps identify when you need breaks

## 📝 Part 2: Working on Tasks

### Starting a New Task

**The Simple Way:**

1. Make sure you're clocked in for a client
2. Type what you're working on (e.g., "Design new homepage")
3. Click "Start Task"
4. A settings window appears

**The Task Settings Window:**

Think of this as filling out a recipe card for your task:

- **Task Type:** 
  - "Quick Task" (15-30 min) - Like making coffee
  - "Standard Task" (30-90 min) - Like cooking dinner
  - "Deep Work" (90+ min) - Like preparing a feast

- **Goal Duration:** 
  - How long you plan to spend (optional)
  - System will remind you at key milestones (20%, 50%, 90%, etc.)

- **Task Intent:**
  - Why you're doing this task
  - Examples: "Build," "Fix," "Learn," "Research"

- **Categories:**
  - Tags to organize your work
  - Examples: "Design," "Code," "Meeting," "Email"

**What You See While Working:**

- A big card showing your active task
- A live timer counting up (minutes and seconds)
- Your task description
- Fields to add notes, links, and screenshots
- Buttons to Pause or Complete the task

### Task Priority (IMPORTANT!)

**Before completing any task, you MUST select a priority:**

- **🔴 Immediate Impact Task** - Urgent, critical work (client emergencies)
- **🟢 Daily Task** - Things you do every day
- **🔵 Weekly Task** - Tasks you do once a week
- **🟣 Monthly Task** - Things you do once a month
- **🌿 Evergreen Task** - Ongoing work with no deadline
- **🔶 Trigger-Based Task** - Reactive work (responding to emails, fixing bugs)

**Why This Matters:**
- The system won't let you complete a task without selecting a priority
- This data helps your manager understand what type of work you spend time on
- Helps you see patterns in your own workload

### Pausing a Task

**When to Pause:**
- You need to switch to another task
- Taking a break
- Got interrupted
- Need to work on something else temporarily

**What Happens:**
1. Click "Pause Task"
2. The timer stops immediately
3. Your work time is saved accurately
4. The task moves to your "Paused Tasks" list
5. You can start a different task or take a break

**The 30-Minute Warning:**
- If a task stays paused for over 30 minutes, you'll see a gentle alert
- This helps you remember to come back to it
- The alert appears in a pastel pink box at the top of your screen

### Resuming a Task

**How to Resume:**
1. Find your paused task in the list
2. Click "Resume"
3. The timer starts from where you left off
4. All your previous notes and settings are restored

**Behind the Scenes:**
- The system remembers exactly how long you worked before pausing
- When you resume, it adds new time to your previous time
- This gives you 100% accurate total time for the task

### Completing a Task

**The Checklist Before You Can Complete:**

✅ **Comments Required** - You must write notes about what you did  
✅ **Priority Required** - You must select a task priority  
✅ **Optional but Helpful:**
  - Add a link to your work
  - Upload screenshots showing what you accomplished
  - Select a task status (In Progress, Completed, Blocked, On Hold)

**What Happens When You Complete:**

1. Click "Complete Task"
2. The system calculates your exact total time (including all paused periods)
3. A completion dialog shows you a summary
4. After 1 second, a "Task Enjoyment" popup appears
5. Rate how much you enjoyed this task (1-5 stars)
6. Task moves to your "Completed Tasks" list

**Why Rate Enjoyment?**
- Helps identify what kind of work you love vs. tolerate
- Your manager can see if you're doing work that energizes you
- System learns what tasks to recommend or rotate

## 📋 Part 3: The Task Queue System

### What Is the Queue?

Think of it like a to-do list or a prep station in a kitchen. Before you start cooking (working), you gather all your ingredients (tasks) and line them up.

### Adding Tasks to Your Queue

**Method 1: While Viewing a Client**

1. Make sure you've selected a client
2. Type a task description
3. Click "Add to Queue" (instead of "Start Task")
4. Task is saved for later

**Method 2: From a Template (see next section)**

**What You See:**
- A "Queue (X)" button showing how many tasks are waiting
- Click it to expand and see all queued tasks
- Each task shows:
  - The task description
  - When you added it
  - A "Start" button to begin working
  - A "Delete" button to remove it

### Starting a Task from Queue

1. Open your queue list
2. Find the task you want to work on
3. Click the "▶️ Start" button next to it
4. The task settings window opens (just like starting a new task)
5. Fill in the settings and start working
6. Task is removed from queue automatically

**Pro Tip:**
Add all your tasks to the queue in the morning, then work through them one by one. It's like meal prepping for your workday!

## ✨ Part 4: Recurring Task Templates

### What Are Templates?

Templates are like saved recipes for tasks you do regularly. Instead of typing the same thing over and over, you save it once and reuse it.

**Perfect For:**
- Daily standup meetings
- Weekly reports
- Monthly invoicing
- Regular email checks
- Routine maintenance tasks

### Creating a Template

1. Click "New Template" in the Templates section
2. Fill out the template form:
   - **Template Name:** What you'll call it (e.g., "Daily Email Check")
   - **Description:** What the task involves
   - **Default Client:** Which client this is usually for (optional)
   - **Default Task Type:** Quick, Standard, or Deep Work (optional)
   - **Default Categories:** Tags like "Email," "Admin" (optional)
   - **Default Priority:** Daily, Weekly, etc. (optional)
   - **Auto-Queue Toggle:** Should this be added to your queue automatically? (future feature)

3. Click "Save Template"

### Using Templates

**What You See:**
- Each template appears as a beautiful card
- Shows the template name, description, and default settings
- Three buttons on each card:
  - **➕ Add to Queue** - Adds this task to your queue
  - **✏️ Edit** - Modify the template
  - **🗑 Delete** - Remove the template

**How to Use:**
1. Click "Add to Queue" on any template
2. A copy of that task is added to your queue
3. The task includes all the default settings you specified
4. A green notification confirms it was added
5. Go to your queue and start the task when ready

**Example Workflow:**

Morning routine:
1. Open Templates section
2. Click "Add to Queue" on "Daily Email Check"
3. Click "Add to Queue" on "Morning Standup"
4. Click "Add to Queue" on "Client Updates"
5. Open your Queue
6. Work through each task one by one

## 🔔 Part 6: The Notification System

### Task Milestone Reminders

When you set a goal duration for a task, you'll get cheerful reminders:

- **20%** - "Great start!"
- **40%** - "Keep going!"
- **50%** - "Halfway there!"
- **60%** - "Making progress!"
- **75%** - "Almost there!"
- **80%** - "Final stretch!"
- **90%** - "Nearly done!"
- **100%** - "Goal reached! 🎉"
- **110%** - "Running over - consider wrapping up"
- **120%** - "Significantly over goal"

**These Are Unlimited:**
You'll get ALL milestone notifications for tasks with goals. They don't count toward the notification limit.

### Mood & Energy Check-Ins

**Frequency:**
- First check: 2 seconds after clocking in
- Regular checks: Every 30 minutes

**The 5-Per-Hour Limit:**
To avoid notification fatigue, mood and energy checks are limited to 5 per hour total. Task milestone reminders are unlimited because they're directly related to your work.

### Paused Task Alerts

If a task has been paused for over 30 minutes, you'll see a gentle reminder at the top of your screen. It's not pushy - just a friendly nudge!

## 📤 Part 7: Submitting Your Daily Report

### When to Submit

At the end of your workday, you'll submit your EOD (End of Day) report. This packages all your completed tasks into a professional summary.

### What Gets Included

- All completed tasks for the day
- Total hours worked per client
- Comments and notes for each task
- Any screenshots you uploaded
- Your mood and energy check-in data (for your personal analytics)
- Task priorities and categories

### How to Submit

1. Make sure all your tasks are completed (or paused for tomorrow)
2. Scroll to the bottom of the page
3. Click "Submit DAR" button
4. A confirmation appears
5. An email is automatically sent to your manager
6. Your report is archived in the History section

**Important:**
- You can submit even if you're still clocked in (but you'll get a warning)
- Once submitted, you can still view the report in History
- You can submit multiple times per day if needed

## 🔐 Part 9: Privacy & Security

### Your Data Is Protected

**What You Can See:**
- Your own tasks, time entries, and reports
- Your own mood and energy check-ins
- Your own templates and queue

**What You Cannot See:**
- Other users' tasks or time entries
- Other users' personal data
- Company-wide information (unless you're an admin)

### Admin Privileges

If you're a manager/admin:
- You can view any team member's dashboard
- You can see team-wide metrics
- You can filter by user and client
- All data is still secure and only visible to authorized admins

### Auto Clock-Out Protection

**The 12-Hour Rule:**
If you forget to clock out, the system automatically clocks you out after 12 hours. This prevents:
- Accidentally tracking time overnight
- Inflated time reports
- Database issues with stale sessions

## ❓ Part 11: Troubleshooting & FAQ

### "I can't complete a task!"

**Check:**
- ✅ Did you add comments? (Required)
- ✅ Did you select a priority? (Required)

### "My time looks wrong!"

**Remember:**
- Time is calculated from all your work sessions
- Paused time is NOT counted toward your total
- The system uses accumulated seconds for accuracy
- Check if you resumed a paused task (time adds up from all sessions)

### "Where did my paused task go?"

Look in the "Paused Tasks" section below your active task. Click to expand it.

### "I clocked in by mistake!"

Click "Clock Out" immediately. The system will record a very short session, which you can ignore or delete.

### "My template isn't working!"

**Check:**
- Is the template saved? (Look for it in the list)
- Did you click "Add to Queue" or just viewed it?
- Check your Queue list - the task should be there

### "I forgot to clock in!"

Unfortunately, you can't backdate clock-ins. Start clocking in now, and your manager can manually adjust your time if needed.

### "The app is slow or not loading!"

1. Refresh your browser
2. Check your internet connection
3. Try logging out and back in
4. Contact your IT admin if problems persist

## 🎓 Part 13: Understanding the Metrics

### Efficiency Score

**What It Measures:**
How much actual work time vs. total time (including pauses)

**Example:**
- You worked 4 hours total
- You paused for 1 hour total
- Your efficiency is 80% (4 out of 5 hours were active work)

**What's Good:**
- 80-100% = Excellent
- 60-79% = Good
- 40-59% = Needs improvement
- Below 40% = Too many distractions

### Focus Score

**What It Measures:**
How well you concentrate based on:
- Task types you choose (Deep Work scores higher)
- Your mood and energy levels
- Task enjoyment ratings
- How often you switch tasks

**What's Good:**
- 80-100% = Excellent focus
- 60-79% = Good focus
- 40-59% = Scattered
- Below 40% = Very distracted

### Task Velocity

**What It Measures:**
How many "productivity points" you earn based on:
- Quick Tasks = 1 point
- Standard Tasks = 2 points
- Deep Work = 3 points

**Example:**
- 3 Quick Tasks = 3 points
- 2 Standard Tasks = 4 points
- 1 Deep Work = 3 points
- **Total Velocity: 10 points/day**

### Work Rhythm

**What It Measures:**
Your consistency throughout the day. Are you working steadily or in bursts?

**What It Considers:**
- Time of day patterns
- Break frequency
- Session lengths
- Mood/energy stability

### Energy Level

**What It Measures:**
Your overall energy based on:
- Your self-reported energy checks
- How long your work sessions are
- Time between tasks (recovery time)
- Whether you're in "flow state"

## 📖 Glossary

**Active Task** - A task you're currently working on  
**Accumulated Seconds** - Total time worked on a task across all sessions  
**Clock-In** - Recording when you start working for a client  
**Deep Work** - Complex tasks requiring intense focus  
**EOD** - End of Day  
**EST** - Eastern Standard Time (timezone used for consistency)  
**Paused Task** - A task temporarily stopped but not completed  
**Priority** - The importance/type category of a task  
**Queue** - Your list of prepared tasks ready to start  
**Quick Task** - Simple tasks taking 15-30 minutes  
**RLS** - Row Level Security (keeps your data private)  
**Standard Task** - Regular tasks taking 30-90 minutes  
**Template** - A saved recipe for recurring tasks  
**Time Entry** - A single recorded work session
